Meiji Jingu

Meiji Jingu, nestled in the heart of Tokyo’s Shibuya district, is one of Japan’s most significant and revered Shinto shrines. Dedicated to Emperor Meiji and Empress Shoken, the shrine was completed in 1920 to honor their legacy and contributions to Japan’s modernization. Despite being located in a bustling metropolis, Meiji Jingu is surrounded by a vast, tranquil forest of over 100,000 trees, offering a serene escape from the city’s energy.

The shrine’s main structures, built in traditional nagare-zukuri architectural style, exude an understated elegance, with cypress wood and copper roofs that blend harmoniously with the natural surroundings. A massive torii gate marks the entrance, leading visitors along a peaceful gravel path toward the shrine’s inner sanctuary.

Visitors to Meiji Jingu can take part in traditional Shinto practices, such as writing wishes on Ema (wooden plaques) or observing a Shinto wedding procession, a common sight at the shrine. Adjacent to the shrine, the Meiji Jingu Inner Garden offers seasonal beauty, including a famous iris garden that was a favorite of the imperial couple.

A symbol of Japan’s cultural and spiritual heritage, Meiji Jingu continues to be a place of deep reverence, where history, nature, and tradition intertwine in the heart of modern Tokyo.
